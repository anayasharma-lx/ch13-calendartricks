Why the magic works
Mind reader mode
A friend picks a hidden block and tells you the sum. Enter it to instantly find their top-left number.

The secret: every date in a block can be written as a + offset, where a is the top-left date. The offsets always add to the same constant — so sum = n·a + constant. Given the sum, you solve for a instantly.

Why it works — algebra
Let the number = 10a + b Reversed = 10b + a Difference = (10b+a) - (10a+b) = 9b - 9a = 9(b-a) → always ÷ 9 ✓ Sum = (10a+b) + (10b+a) = 11(a+b) → always ÷ 11 ✓
The magic rule
Pick any 2-digit number where the digits differ. Add it to its reverse → always divisible by 11. Subtract the smaller from the larger → always divisible by 9. Both facts hold for EVERY such number — not luck, just algebra.

Digit tricks work because our number system is base 10. A two-digit number is 10a + b. When you reverse digits you get 10b + a. The difference is 9(b−a), the sum is 11(a+b). Both are algebraic identities — they hold without exception.
